Juan Cuadrado is a Colombian professional footballer. The young athlete currently plays for the Italian giants Juventus in the Italian league Serie A. He has also played in many other clubs including Chelsea FC and Fiorentina as a midfielder. He was listed in the preliminary squad of Colombia for the 2018 FIFA World Cup. To know more about him, you can read the below article.

Who are the parents Juan Cuadrado?

Going back to his early life, Juan Cuadrado was born as Juan Guillermo Cuadrado Bello to Marcela Bello Guerrero and Guillermo Cuadrado in 1988. He grew up in Necocli, Colombia with her sister, Maria Angel. His nationality is Columbian and his ethnicity is Black. His birth sign is Gemini.

When did Juan Cuadrado begin his sports career?

  • Juan Cuadrado started his junior career by joining the local youth teams of Colombia such as Atletico Uraba and Independiente Medellin. 
  • Later, He began his professional career by joining the senior team of Independiente Medellin in 2008 and made his debut and scored his first goal for the club against Boyaca Chico.
  • He then signed with the Italian club, Udinese Calcio in early July 2009 against Chievo and was transferred to the fellow Italian side, Cesena as a  loan in 2011.
  • After that, he signed with Fiorentina for a €1 million temporary deal during the summer of 2012  and scored his first goal against Cagliari.
  • Also, he has signed a four and a  half year deal with English Premier League side, Chelsea managed by Jose Mourinho during February 2015 for a starting transfer fee of £23.3 million that could rose to £ 26.8 million and was a part of the team along with Spanish footballer Cesc Fabregas and Nigerian midfielder, John Obi Mikel that went on to win Premier League in 2014/15.
  • He was then transferred to the Italian football club, Juventus FC  during 2015/16 and 2016/17 and is currently playing for the club captained by legendary Italian goalkeeper, Gianluigi Buffon whom he signed a permanent contract deal which was worth €20 million.
  • During the 2019-20 season, under the club’s new manager Maurizio Sarri, He was often deployed as an attacking right-back. On 3rd November, he made his 150th appearance for the club in a 1-0 away win over cross-city rivals Torino in the Derby Della Mole. Later that month, he extended his contract with the club until 2022.
