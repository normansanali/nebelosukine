Lexington KY Shooting Victims

Tragedy struck Hollow Creek Road in Lexington on a fateful Saturday afternoon when a shooting outside an apartment complex claimed the lives of a man and a woman.

The Lexington Police Department swiftly responded to the distressing scene at 522 Hollow Creek Rd. at approximately 3:58 p.m., where they discovered the two individuals suffering from gunshot wounds.

Preliminary investigations by the police, led by Lt. Paul Boyles, point to a grim scenario, as they believe it was a murder-suicide.

The woman, identified as Christina Fikes, aged 23, was pronounced deceased at the scene.

The man involved in the tragic incident was rushed to the emergency room at the University of Kentucky Chandler Hospital, where, regrettably, he too was pronounced dead, as confirmed by Fayette County Coroner Gary Ginn.

The investigation into this heart-wrenching incident is ongoing, with both victims scheduled for autopsies in Louisville to shed further light on the circumstances surrounding their untimely deaths.

Coroner Ginn disclosed that an altercation preceded the shooting, emphasizing that the police are diligently working to piece together the complete narrative.

Authorities have communicated with various individuals, including at least one eyewitness, as they strive to unravel the details of this tragic event.

This marks the third fatal shooting in Lexington in a single week and the second on that very Saturday, underscoring the urgency of addressing the escalating issue of gun violence in the community.
