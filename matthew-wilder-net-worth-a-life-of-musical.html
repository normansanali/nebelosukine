Matthew Wilder Net Worth: A Life of Musical Success and Astute Investments

September 2024 · 4 minute read

Matthew Wilder is a name that has long been synonymous with the world of music. Born on January 24, 1953, in Manhattan, this American musician, record producer, singer-songwriter, and composer has had an illustrious career both as a folk singer in the ’70s and as a prominent producer and songwriter in subsequent years. Over the decades, he has created some timeless musical classics that still resonate deeply with listeners today.

Wilder rose to fame as part of Matthew & Peter in the early 70s. The duo released one album called “Under the Arch,” which showcased their rich vocal harmonies beautifully. However, it was not until he moved to Los Angeles at the end of the decade that he found success singing for TV commercials and backing established artists like Rickie Lee Jones and Bette Midler.

In 1981 he signed with Clive Davis’ Arista Records, marking a significant turning point in his career. Two years later, Wilder released his debut solo album “I Don’t Speak the Language,” which peaked at #49 on Billboard 200 chart. The standout single from that album, “Break My Stride,” became a global hit reaching #5 on Billboard Hot 100 chart; it’s been featured countless times across movies and TV shows since.

Despite continued success with other singles like “The Kid’s American,” Wilder faced setbacks after his second LP’s release ‘Bouncin’ Off The Walls’, which didn’t perform as expected.

However, Wilder’s talent soon caught up again when he transitioned towards production and songwriting for other artists. He produced albums for some influential artists like No Doubt’s smash breakthrough album “Tragic Kingdom”, Kelly Clarkson’s iconic hits- ‘Breakaway’ , Christina Aguilera’s critically acclaimed self-titled debut album among many others throughout his career. He also wrote Aguilera’s song ‘Reflection’ for Disney’s Mulan animation movie soundtrack responsible for creating one of the most iconic ballads in Disney history.
