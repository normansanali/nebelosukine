JetBlue Airline Bans Olivia Culpo's Dog

July 2024 · 4 minute read

Influencer and model Olivia Culpo tearfully criticized JetBlue Airways after her dog was not allowed on her flight and has faced the worst experience.

The former Miss USA was brought to tears, forcing her to cancel her flight home to see her parents, who are battling against COVID-19.

Olivia Cuplo uploaded a video on her Instagram story, saying:

'I'm sure I'm not the only one who ran away crying at the airport, but it's the worst experience I've ever had, I just want to be able to go home."

She later shared another post on her Instagram, which showed her tears running down her face as she is standing at the airport, saying:

"When both your parents have COVID and you're trying to get home to help them but suddenly your dog who was already pre-approved is not allowed to fly with you. I have never wanted to be this person and I have always been the hugest @jetblue fan but I am just soooo so so upset right now. He has flown on this airline so many times and it's never been an issue."

She also shared two throwback photos showing her dog on two separate JetBlue flights in May.

"On another JetBlue flight that month….I just really don't get it."

She captioned the second pic of the pup.

According to foxbusiness.com, the JetBlue airline had not explained why the dog could not board the flight like before but suggested it may have had to do with the dog not being a trained service animal.

JetBlue issued the following statement,

"JetBlue is committed to assisting customers who have a need to use trained service animals while traveling. At the same time, we are committed to following updated federal regulations prohibiting customers from bringing untrained or unqualified animals on board, including attempts to claim a pet is a trained service animal. At any time while traveling, our crewmembers may ask legally allowed questions and confirm the qualifications of a trained service animal. We work to consistently enforce these policies, however, a customer may be stopped from proceeding with travel at any point if it is determined an animal does not meet the required qualifications."

Culpo later appeared back at her home, and she was seen practicing the cello.
