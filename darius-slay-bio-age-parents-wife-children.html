Darius Slay Biography

After high school, Slay attended Itawamba Community College in Fulton, Mississippi, where he played football for two seasons. He continued to improve each season, and in 2014 he was named a second-team All-Pro after recording 70 tackles, two interceptions, and a league-leading 26 passes defensed.

Slay earned his first Pro Bowl selection in 2015 after recording 59 tackles, two interceptions, and a league-leading 13 passes defensed. He was named a second-team All-Pro for the second straight year. In 2016, Slay signed a four-year contract extension with the Lions worth $50.2 million.

Slay continued to be a standout player for the Lions, earning Pro Bowl selections in 2017 and 2018. He was also named a first-team All-Pro in 2017 after recording eight interceptions, 26 passes defensed, and 60 tackles.

On March 19, 2020, Slay was traded to the Philadelphia Eagles in exchange for a third-round pick in the 2020 NFL Draft and a fifth-round pick in the 2020 NFL Draft. He signed a three-year, $50 million contract extension with the Eagles on April 2, 2020.
