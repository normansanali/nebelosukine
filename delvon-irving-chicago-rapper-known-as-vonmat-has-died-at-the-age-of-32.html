Vonmar’s Rise to Fame

As a member of the Chicago rap collective Thot Boyz, Vonmar achieved fame. He gained recognition for his daring attempts to imitate the outrageous stunts seen on the popular television series “Jackass.” One of Vonmar’s most widely circulated pranks was the “Put Em’ in a Coffin” stunt, where participants would hurl themselves onto flat surfaces or crowded tables, mimicking lying in a coffin.

The Vine video featuring the “Put Em’ in a Coffin” prank went viral back in 2014, amassing an impressive 2.9 million views, 62,300 likes, and 62,000 shares. This craze led others to replicate the stunt online, using hashtags such as #PutEmInACoffin and #PutEmInTheCoffin. However, Vonmar’s involvement in the prank led to legal trouble in 2015 when he was arrested and charged for performing it at a 7-Eleven store.

Legal Troubles

On New Year’s Day of 2015, Vonmar and a companion entered a 7-Eleven store and executed the “Put Em’ in a Coffin” stunt. They energetically leaped onto a merchandise rack, recorded the entire episode, and proceeded to disrupt the store while loudly proclaiming the catchphrase.

The store clerk promptly contacted the authorities, and the duo made off with a coffee urn, though they ultimately left it behind. Vonmar later posted a Vine video documenting the incident, which quickly gained widespread attention on various social media platforms. Consequently, he found himself in legal trouble, leading to his arrest and subsequent guilty plea on charges of felony theft. The court sentenced him to two years of probation and a 60-day stint in county jail.
