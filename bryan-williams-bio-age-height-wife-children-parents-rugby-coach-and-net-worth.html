Bryan Williams Rugby Union

Williams started his rugby career in high school. Williams distinguished himself in the 1970 South African Rugby Tour, scoring 14 tries in his 13 appearances. His international rugby career lasted from 1970 to 1978 in which he played 113 matches and scored 66 tries in all matches as an All Black, which was a record until beaten by John Kirwan.

Following his retirement from rugby, he coached a number of club sides in New Zealand. From the 1990s onwards, he has been the national rugby coach for Samoa. In 2011, he was appointed President of the NZRU.
